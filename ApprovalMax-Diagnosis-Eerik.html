ApprovalMax — Growth Diagnosis
Prepared for Eerik Beeton, Director of Digital and Demand Generation

The Product Works. The Acquisition System Does Not.

ApprovalMax has 19,000 paying businesses, review scores competitors envy, and a Xero App Store full of five-star ratings. The paid acquisition side is a different picture — spend is going out, but the system to capture what comes back is not in place.

The Core Leak

Finance teams searching "Xero approval workflow" or "accounts payable automation UK" right now are not landing on a conversion experience built to close them. They are landing on pages that were not designed around their decision-making process. Some will try the product. Most will keep looking. A portion will end up at BILL or Lightyear before ApprovalMax ever knew they were in market.

The core problem is not the ads themselves. It is that the components needed to make paid acquisition work — attribution tracking, landing pages matched to search intent, conversion rate data, retargeting sequences — have not been built yet. The ApprovalMax blog ranks for accounts payable automation comparison terms. That means buyers are finding ApprovalMax through content, reading about it, comparing it to BILL and Tipalti — and then converting somewhere else. The organic side is working. The paid side is not set up to catch them.

Spend is going out. The system to capture what comes back is not in place. That is not a criticism of the team — it is the starting condition for a build-from-scratch mandate. And it means every pound spent right now is returning less than it should.
What This Seems to Indicate

The attribution layer does not exist yet.

Customer acquisition cost, payback period, and funnel conversion from trial to demo to closed deal cannot be read cleanly right now. Every pound spent on Google Ads or LinkedIn is going into a black box. You cannot double down on what works because you cannot see what works.

Ad accounts have been running without strategic direction.

A pay per click specialist has been executing in-platform. But in-platform execution without a strategic layer above it produces accounts built around activity, not conversion. The structure, bidding approach, and audience segmentation likely need rebuilding before the data can be trusted.

Finance teams are converting elsewhere on intent ApprovalMax already generated.

Organic content is working — ranking for comparison terms, bringing buyers in. But without paid retargeting and intent-capture infrastructure, those buyers are reading and leaving. The content investment is not being fully converted.

The landing pages are not doing the job of a sales conversation.

Hotjar and Clarity have not been used to track visitor behaviour in a systematic way. Nobody knows where finance teams stop reading, what makes them click away before the trial, or which message is closest to converting them. The pages are running on assumptions, not data.

LinkedIn Ads are likely burning budget on the wrong offer.

Finance teams do not respond to generic software promotion. They respond to specific, credibility-led content. Without a tested offer framework for a financial controls audience, LinkedIn spend is producing impressions, not conversations. The issue is not the channel — it is the offer and the creative strategy behind it.

BILL is running aggressive paid campaigns in the United States and capturing small and medium business finance teams before they ever discover ApprovalMax. Lightyear is active in the United Kingdom and Australia — the same Xero-native markets where ApprovalMax is strongest. Nanonets is capturing artificial intelligence invoice processing search intent, a fast-growing category.

The window where ApprovalMax could own the Xero-native paid acquisition space is now. But it closes as competitors spend more and as the cost of ranking for "accounts payable automation UK" increases. Every month without a working conversion system is not neutral — it is ground lost to competitors who are spending systematically. The attribution layer, paid search structure, and conversion framework need to be in place regardless. If those foundations are not built now, another quarter passes before the function produces output.

What I Would Fix First
1

Rebuild the attribution layer before anything else.

Good decisions about paid acquisition cannot be made without reliable conversion tracking. Google Analytics 4 needs to be connected to HubSpot with clean trial-to-demo-to-closed tracking. This is the prerequisite for everything that follows. Without it, every other decision is a guess.

Unlocks: Real cost per trial, cost per demo, and cost per closed deal — the numbers that make the paid acquisition case legible to leadership.
2

Restructure Google Ads around intent, not brand.

Finance teams searching "approval workflow for Xero" or "accounts payable automation UK" are in active buying mode. Restructuring around high-intent, product-specific terms — rather than broad brand awareness — produces trials from people who already want what ApprovalMax does. Most of the budget is likely on the wrong terms right now.

Unlocks: A reliable cost per trial number that can be used to justify expanding the budget to leadership.
3

Fix the click-to-trial journey before scaling spend.

The gap between someone clicking an ad and starting a trial is where most of the money disappears. Landing pages need to be matched to the specific search term or ad creative that sent the visitor. A finance manager who clicked "accounts payable software for Xero" needs to land on a page that speaks directly to that context, not a generic product overview.

Unlocks: Lower cost per trial and higher trial quality, because visitors who convert are self-selected on intent rather than general curiosity.
4

Build one tested LinkedIn offer before scaling the channel.

Rather than running multiple LinkedIn campaigns without a proven offer, pick one audience segment — United Kingdom-based Finance Controllers at companies with 50 to 200 employees using Xero — and one offer: a specific case study or benchmark report that speaks to their situation. Test that combination until it converts. Then replicate.

Unlocks: A LinkedIn playbook that works for finance audiences — something most competitors in this space have not cracked yet.
5

Set up a retargeting sequence to catch the comparison shoppers.

Buyers who visit ApprovalMax after reading a competitor comparison post are warm. They are evaluating options. A targeted retargeting sequence showing specific proof — "28 hours saved per month, 80% reduction in manual errors at Paddle Australia" — hits them at the right moment with the right message before they convert elsewhere.

Unlocks: Recovery of intent that was already generated by the content team but was leaking out of the funnel before it could convert.

Right now, paid acquisition at ApprovalMax is activity without a system. Ads are running. Spend is going out. But the data coming back cannot be read, and the journey from click to trial has friction nobody has measured yet. The shift is from running campaigns to running a conversion system. When attribution is clean, when landing pages are matched to intent, and when the trial journey removes friction at the known drop-off points, the same ad spend produces significantly more trials. And those trials, because they are better qualified, convert to demos at a higher rate. The whole commercial case for paid acquisition becomes legible — and fundable.
